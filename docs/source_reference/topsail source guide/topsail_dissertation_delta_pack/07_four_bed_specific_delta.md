# 07 - Four-bed specific dissertation delta

This file extracts only dissertation evidence relevant to extending beyond the paper's two-bed implementation. It is intentionally stricter than the earlier extraction pack because the dissertation uses stronger wording: current toPSAil does not support more than two adsorbers. Optimism is not a software architecture.

## Four-bed interpretation rules

- **Treat four-bed support as a new feature, not a UI tweak.** Source: Diss p. 243 (PDF p. 278), §6.2.4 says current toPSAil "does not support simulations involving adsorbent bed layering, more than two adsorbers".
- **Predefined step interactions are two-adsorber scoped.** Source: Diss p. 239 (PDF p. 274), §6.1 says the available step list "only considers up to two-adsorber interactions".
- **Core models may generalize, but connections and costs are the hard part.** Source: Diss p. 243 (PDF p. 278), §6.2.4 says underlying models can generalize with "minor adjustments", but >2 adsorbers bring "combinatorially increasing possible connections" and computational costs.
- **Use fixed-duration schedules before multi-bed events unless an event policy is explicit.** Source: Diss p. 235-236 (PDF p. 270-271), §5.5.3 says no general approach is known for conflicting multi-adsorber event objectives. [INFERENCE]
- **Shared tanks are active dynamic participants.** Source: Diss p. 137-139 (PDF p. 172-174), §3.2.5 formulates feed/product tank streams with `n_a` adsorbers; Diss p. 212 (PDF p. 247), §4.2.4 says tank flows follow adsorber interactions and valve models.
